Pokér

Pokémon × Poker · Quick Rule Sheet
A card game by Owen

Objective

Build the best 5-card hand from standard playing cards. Win Pokémon cards through betting and claim the highest score when the round ends.

What You Need

  • 1 standard deck of playing cards (Jokers optional)
  • Pokémon cards — used as currency
  • 2 or more players

Setup

  • Place 10 playing cards face down in the center (2 rows of 5).
  • Deal each player 5 playing cards (kept hidden).
  • Each player chooses 6 Pokémon cards from their collection (3 Basic, 2 Stage 1, 1 Special), placed face up in front of them (3-card row over a 3-card row).

Pokémon Card Values

Basic Pokémon1 PP
Stage 1 Pokémon2 PP
Special (V, holo, etc.)3 PP

PP = PokéPoints. Each player's 6 cards = 3 Basic, 2 Stage 1, 1 Special.

Betting

  • Bet immediately after receiving your 5 cards.
  • Each turn: match the highest bet, raise it, or fold.
  • If someone bets 2 PP, everyone must bet 2 PP or more to stay in.
  • All bet cards go to the central pot and are locked in — no take-backs.

Center Cards (Drafting)

  • Players take turns flipping center cards one at a time.
  • When a card flips, anyone may call “Dibs!”
  • The first valid Dibs claims the card — even if you misread it, you must take it.
  • If no one claims it, it stays face up as an option.

Hand Limit & Swap

  • You must always hold exactly 5 cards.
  • When you take a card: add it, then choose 1 card to remove.
  • Place the removed card face up in the spot the claimed card came from.

Stealing

  • Any face-up replacement card may be taken by another player.
  • They must swap one of their own cards into that same spot.
  • Hand size always stays at 5.

Scoring — No Stacking

Score only your single best combination.

Runs (cards in sequence)
2 in a row5
3 in a row10
4 in a row15
5 in a row20
Suits (same suit)
2 of a suit5
3 of a suit10
4 of a suit15
5 of a suit20
SPECIAL   Both Jokers = 50 · Perfect Run (5 in order, same suit) = 50

End & Winning

  • The round ends when all 10 center cards are flipped and resolved.
  • Highest-scoring hand wins and takes the whole Pokémon pot.
  • Tiebreaker: If scores are tied, highest single card wins.

The Bank

  • Pokémon cards you win go into your Bank — kept separate from your active 6.
  • If you run out of usable Pokémon cards, refill from your Bank.
Remember
Always 5 cards in hand · “Dibs” is final · no stacking scores · swapped cards go face up in the center spot they came from.

Pokér

Full Rules & Instructions
A card game by Owen

Pokér is a hybrid of poker, drafting, and real-time claiming. Standard playing cards decide who wins each round; Pokémon cards are the betting currency and the prize.

1Objective

Score the highest total by building the best 5-card hand from standard playing cards, while using Pokémon cards as a betting and reward system.

2Materials

  • 1 standard deck of playing cards (including Jokers if used)
  • Pokémon cards, used as currency
  • At least 2 players

3Setup

  • Place 10 playing cards face down in the center, in two rows of five.
  • Deal each player 5 playing cards, kept hidden from others.
  • Each player chooses 6 Pokémon cards from their collection: 3 Basic, 2 Stage 1, 1 Special.
  • Each player places their Pokémon face up — a 3-card row over a 3-card row — visible to all.

4Pokémon as Currency

Basic Pokémon1 PP
Stage 1 Pokémon2 PP
Special (V, holo, etc.)3 PP

All Pokémon bet into the pot cannot be taken back.

5Betting

Betting begins immediately after players receive their 5 playing cards. Each player must:

  • Match the current highest bet, or
  • Raise the bet (bet more PP), or
  • Fold and sit out the round.

If a player bets 2 PP, all others must bet 2 PP or more to remain in the round.

6Center Card System

  • Players take turns flipping one of the 10 center cards.
  • When a card flips, players may call “Dibs” to claim it.
  • The first valid Dibs call wins the card.
  • A claimed card must be taken even if the player misread it.
  • You must keep exactly 5 cards in hand at all times.

7Swap Rule

When you take a card from the center:

  • Add the card to your hand.
  • Choose one card from your hand to remove.
  • Place the removed card face up in the exact spot the claimed card was flipped.

8Stealing Rule

  • Any face-up replacement card on the table may be taken by another player.
  • They immediately replace it with one card from their own hand, in the same spot.
  • Players always maintain exactly 5 cards in hand.

9Scoring — No Stacking

Score only ONE category per hand — your highest valid score.

Runs
2 consecutive cards5
3 consecutive cards10
4 consecutive cards15
5 consecutive cards20
Suits
2 cards of same suit5
3 cards of same suit10
4 cards of same suit15
5 cards of same suit20
SPECIAL COMBINATIONS
Both Jokers = 50  ·  Perfect Run (5 consecutive, same suit) = 50

10Round End & Winning

  • The round ends when all 10 center cards have been flipped and resolved.
  • Each player scores their final 5-card hand.
  • The highest score wins the round and collects all Pokémon cards in the pot.
  • Tiebreaker: If two or more players have the same score, the player with the highest single card wins.

11Bank System

  • Pokémon cards won in the pot go into the player's Bank.
  • The Bank is a separate pile from your active Pokémon.
  • If you run out of usable Pokémon cards, refill from your Bank.
Key Rules Summary
Always hold exactly 5 playing cards · “Dibs” is final and cannot be reversed · all swaps go face up in the original center position · scores never stack · Pokémon cards are only for betting and banking.
